AFP WEST PHILIPPINE SEA (WPS) PROTOCOLS
=======================================
Reference: AFP Western Command Standing Operating Procedures
Governing Law: UNCLOS (1982), RA 9522 (Philippine Baselines Law), 2016 Arbitral Award

---

LEGAL BASIS

The West Philippine Sea refers to the portions of the South China Sea lying within the Philippines'
Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) and continental shelf, as established under UNCLOS Article 55-75.
The Philippines' EEZ extends 200 nautical miles from its baselines.

The 2016 Arbitral Tribunal (PCA Case No. 2013-19) ruled that:
- China's nine-dash line claim has no legal basis under UNCLOS.
- Bajo de Masinloc (Scarborough Shoal) is within Philippine fishing rights.
- Kalayaan Island Group features are entitled to maritime zones.

AFP personnel must understand: presence in the WPS is lawful and sovereign. Do not cede ground
or yield to intimidation without authorization from higher command.

---

VESSEL ENCOUNTER PROCEDURES

Step 1 — OBSERVE
- Record: vessel type, size, color, flag/markings, hull number, direction of travel, speed.
- Count visible crew. Note any weapons, equipment, or unusual deck activity.
- Note exact time and GPS coordinates of first sighting.

Step 2 — DOCUMENT
- Photograph and video everything. Capture hull numbers, flags, and crew faces if possible.
- Do not delete any footage. All recordings are evidentiary material.
- Log entry in the unit logbook immediately.

Step 3 — REPORT
- Transmit a Contact Report to unit HQ immediately.
- Report to Philippine Coast Guard (PCG) hotline: (02) 527-8481.
- Report format: WHO / WHERE (coordinates) / WHAT (vessel description) / WHEN / ACTIONS TAKEN.

Step 4 — HOLD POSITION
- Do not approach foreign vessels without authorization from AFP Western Command.
- Do not engage in verbal altercations that can escalate.
- Maintain observation posture. Do not show weapons unless in self-defense situation.

---

VESSEL CLASSIFICATION AND RESPONSE

CATEGORY A — PHILIPPINE-FLAGGED VESSELS (Civilian/Fishing)
- Render assistance if in distress.
- Verify registration if suspicious. Coordinate with PCG for boarding authority.

CATEGORY B — FOREIGN COMMERCIAL/RESEARCH VESSELS
- Verify if vessel has Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA) clearance.
- Vessels without clearance within 12nm territorial sea = sovereignty incident.
- Escort to nearest PCG station. Do not use force unless resisted.

CATEGORY C — FOREIGN COAST GUARD / NAVY VESSELS
- Do not approach. Observe and document.
- Maintain safe distance (minimum 500m unless fired upon).
- Report immediately to AFP Western Command and wait for orders.
- Do not engage in radio challenges without authorization.

CATEGORY D — UNKNOWN / SUSPICIOUS VESSELS
- Treat as potentially hostile until confirmed otherwise.
- Increase unit readiness to ALERT status.
- Follow CATEGORY C procedures until classification is confirmed.

---

WATER CANNON / LASER HARASSMENT INCIDENTS

If your unit or vessel is subjected to water cannons, laser dazzlers, or acoustic devices:

1. DO NOT RETALIATE. This is a diplomatic incident, not a combat engagement.
2. Seek cover and protect personnel from water impact and laser eye injury.
3. Document continuously — time, duration, coordinates, vessel ID, injuries.
4. Record crew condition: any personnel hit, injuries, equipment damage.
5. Transmit SPOT REPORT to HQ immediately.
6. Commanding Officer requests MEDEVAC if personnel are injured.
7. DFA will be notified through AFP chain of command for diplomatic protest.

Note: Water cannon use against Philippine government vessels in Philippine EEZ
constitutes a violation of international law. Document meticulously — every recording
supports the legal and diplomatic case.

---

AERIAL INCIDENTS OVER WPS

UNIDENTIFIED AIRCRAFT SIGHTING:
- Log: type (fixed-wing/rotary), color, altitude (estimated), heading, markings.
- Do not fire. Observe and report to AFP Air Defense Command.
- Coordinate with Philippine Air Force (PAF) for identification and intercept authorization.

LOW-FLYING AIRCRAFT / DRONE HARASSMENT:
- Document model/type, direction, loiter time.
- Do not shoot down without authorization — aerial engagement rules apply.
- If drone is surveilling your position, assume position is compromised. Report to HQ.

---

RADIO COMMUNICATIONS IN WPS

- Primary reporting channel: AFP Western Command designated frequency (per unit orders).
- Secondary: PCG Channel 16 (international distress/calling frequency).
- All WPS communications must be logged with DTG (Date-Time Group).
- Use proper AFP radio discipline. Avoid disclosing troop strength, exact positions, or intentions
  in the clear (unencrypted). Use designated code words per unit CEOI.

---

RESUPPLY AND ROTATION MISSIONS

- All resupply missions to WPS outposts must be coordinated with AFP Western Command.
- Vessels on resupply missions must carry valid mission orders.
- If intercepted or blocked by foreign vessels:
  1. Do not force through.
  2. Document the interception completely.
  3. Transmit SPOT REPORT immediately.
  4. Await orders from AFP Western Command before proceeding or withdrawing.
