AFP RULES OF ENGAGEMENT (ROE)
==============================
Reference: AFP General Orders, International Humanitarian Law (IHL), Geneva Conventions
Governing Law: Philippine Constitution (1987), RA 6975, RA 9745, RA 11479

IMPORTANT: These are field reference notes. ROE may be modified by mission-specific orders
from your commander. Mission ROE always supplements but never contradicts Philippine law or IHL.

---

FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

1. MILITARY NECESSITY
   Use force only when necessary to accomplish a lawful military objective.
   Never use more force than the mission requires.

2. DISTINCTION
   Always distinguish between combatants and civilians.
   Never deliberately target civilians, civilian property, or protected persons.
   When in doubt — DO NOT FIRE.

3. PROPORTIONALITY
   Collateral damage to civilians or property must not be excessive relative to the military advantage gained.
   A combatant sheltering in a school does not make the school a valid target for destruction.

4. HUMANITY
   Avoid unnecessary suffering. Use the minimum force needed.
   Wounded and surrendering enemies are protected persons under IHL.

---

USE OF FORCE — GRADUATED RESPONSE

STEP 1 — PRESENCE AND POSTURE
   Maintain a calm, visible, professional military presence.
   Many situations are resolved by posture alone.

STEP 2 — VERBAL COMMANDS
   Issue clear, audible orders in Filipino and/or English.
   Example: "Hinto! Hands up! Do not move!"
   Repeat commands clearly before escalating.

STEP 3 — WARNING SHOTS
   Fire into the air or ground (away from personnel) only when:
   - Verbal commands have failed.
   - Situation allows for warning without creating additional risk.
   - Commanding officer authorizes (or situation is urgent).

STEP 4 — NON-LETHAL FORCE
   Use where available: barrier, physical restraint, baton, pepper spray.
   Prefer non-lethal if threat is not immediately deadly.

STEP 5 — LETHAL FORCE
   Authorized ONLY when:
   (a) You or others face imminent threat of death or serious bodily harm, OR
   (b) The enemy is actively engaging with deadly force, OR
   (c) Higher authority has declared hostile force designation.
   Aim for center mass. Stop when threat is neutralized.

---

SELF-DEFENSE

Individual self-defense is an inherent right that cannot be taken away by any order.
You may use lethal force in self-defense when ALL three conditions are met:
  - HOSTILE INTENT: The person clearly intends to harm you or others.
  - HOSTILE ACT: The person is in the process of committing or has committed a harmful act.
  - IMMINENT DANGER: The threat is immediate — not future or speculative.

Unit self-defense: You may also use force to defend your unit, Filipino civilians under your
protection, and other AFP/PCG personnel being attacked.

---

POSITIVE IDENTIFICATION (PID)

Before engaging any target, you must have Positive Identification:
  - Confirm the target is hostile (armed combatant, active threat).
  - Confirm there is no safer alternative.
  - Confirm collateral damage is acceptable under proportionality.

PID checklist:
  [ ] Target is visibly armed or demonstrably hostile?
  [ ] Target is not surrendering or wounded?
  [ ] No civilians in the immediate engagement area?
  [ ] Fire command from unit leader received (unless immediate self-defense)?

If ANY item cannot be confirmed — DO NOT ENGAGE. Report to commander.

---

PROTECTED PERSONS AND PLACES

NEVER engage the following regardless of orders:
  - Civilians not directly participating in hostilities.
  - Wounded or sick combatants (enemy or friendly).
  - Prisoners of war (POWs) and detainees in custody.
  - Medical personnel wearing Red Cross/Red Crescent markings.
  - Chaplains and religious personnel.
  - Journalists with press credentials (unless directly attacking).

NEVER target the following:
  - Hospitals, medical facilities, ambulances.
  - Churches, mosques, cultural heritage sites.
  - Schools and educational institutions.
  - Water and food supply infrastructure essential to civilians.

Note: Protected status is lost if these locations are used for hostile military purposes,
but you must report to commander before engaging — do not make this decision alone.

---

SURRENDER

Enemy personnel attempting to surrender MUST be accepted.
Signs of surrender: hands raised, white flag, dropping weapon, lying prone with hands visible.

Upon surrender:
  1. Order the person to the ground, hands visible.
  2. Approach with cover — do not approach alone.
  3. Disarm and search for hidden weapons.
  4. Secure with restraints (zip tie, cord).
  5. Segregate from other detainees.
  6. Treat humanely. Provide water and medical care if needed.
  7. Report capture to HQ. Begin processing as EPW (Enemy Prisoner of War).

Firing on a surrendering person is a WAR CRIME under the Geneva Conventions.
Firing on a surrendered and disarmed detainee is MURDER under Philippine law.

---

DETAINEE HANDLING

All detainees — whether combatant, suspect, or civilian — must be treated humanely.

RA 9745 (Anti-Torture Act) STRICTLY PROHIBITS:
  - Physical beating or infliction of pain.
  - Electric shock, burning, suffocation, or drowning.
  - Sexual violence or humiliation.
  - Psychological coercion, threats to family, or deprivation of senses.
  - Prolonged isolation in inhumane conditions.

Detainee rights under RA 7438:
  - Right to remain silent.
  - Right to competent and independent counsel.
  - Right to be informed of charges.
  - Right to communicate with family.

Required upon capture:
  - Record name, rank, unit (if known), date, time, and location of capture.
  - Document any injuries present BEFORE your unit took custody.
  - Transfer to proper detention facility within 36 hours.
  - Do not use detainees for intelligence extraction through coercion.

---

CHECKPOINT OPERATIONS

  1. Establish visible checkpoint with AFP identification and clear stop signals.
  2. Approach vehicles with calm, professional demeanor.
  3. Request identification and purpose of travel.
  4. Search vehicles only with legal authority (warrant, checkpoint authorization, or consent).
  5. Do not detain civilians beyond reasonable screening time without cause.
  6. If armed suspect flees checkpoint: do NOT pursue into civilian areas with firearms. Report and cordon.
  7. Log all vehicles stopped, IDs checked, and incidents at the checkpoint.

---

DUTY TO REPORT

All AFP personnel have a duty to report:
  - Suspected violations of ROE by any soldier, regardless of rank.
  - Suspected war crimes or human rights abuses.
  - Illegal orders from superior officers.

Report through: Chain of Command → AFP Inspector General → Commission on Human Rights (CHR)
You cannot be punished for making a good-faith report of an ROE violation.
Failure to report a known violation may result in criminal liability.

---

AFP CORE VALUES

Courage — Act rightly even when it is dangerous.
Integrity — Do what is right when no one is watching.
Loyalty — To the Constitution, the Filipino people, and your unit.
Patriotism — Service to the nation above self.

AFP Motto: Katarungan, Kalayaan, Kapayapaan
(Justice, Freedom, Peace)
