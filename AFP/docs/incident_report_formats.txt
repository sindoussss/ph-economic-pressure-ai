AFP STANDARD REPORT FORMATS
============================
Reference: AFP G3 (Operations) Standing Operating Procedures
Purpose: Ensure accurate, timely, standardized reporting across all AFP units.

GENERAL RULES FOR ALL REPORTS:
  - Use Date-Time Group (DTG) format: DDMMMYYYY / HHMM (24-hour). Example: 15MAY2025 / 1430H
  - State facts only. No speculation, assumptions, or personal interpretation unless in ASSESSMENT section.
  - Use grid coordinates (MGRS or UTM) for all locations. Landmark as backup only.
  - All reports must identify: WHO is reporting, WHAT happened, WHERE, WHEN, and WHAT WAS DONE.
  - Classify appropriately: UNCLASSIFIED / RESTRICTED / CONFIDENTIAL per content sensitivity.

---

1. SPOT REPORT (SPOTREP)
   Used for: Immediate reporting of significant events (enemy contact, vessel sighting,
   casualty, suspicious activity). Transmitted within minutes of incident.

   FORMAT:
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   SUBJECT: SPOT REPORT — [INCIDENT TYPE IN CAPS]
   CLASSIFICATION: [UNCLASSIFIED / RESTRICTED / CONFIDENTIAL]
   DTG: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   REPORTING UNIT: [Unit Name and Designation]
   REPORT NUMBER: [Sequential number for the reporting period]

   1. SITUATION:
      [Factual description of what occurred. Include: who/what was observed,
      exact location (grid), time of observation, number of personnel/vessels/vehicles,
      description of equipment or weapons, direction of movement, any actions taken by the subject.]

   2. ACTION TAKEN:
      [What your unit did in immediate response. Be specific.]

   3. ASSESSMENT:
      [Your evaluation of the threat level and nature of the incident.]

   4. RECOMMENDATION:
      [What you are requesting from higher command: reinforcement, MEDEVAC, guidance, etc.]

   5. ATTACHMENTS:
      [List photos, video, sketches, captured documents — even if not yet transmitted.]

   REPORTED BY: [Rank, Name, Position]
   CONTACT: [Radio frequency / call sign / phone number]
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

   EXAMPLE:
   SUBJECT: SPOT REPORT — FOREIGN VESSEL SIGHTING
   DTG: 12MAY2025 / 0845H
   REPORTING UNIT: 3rd Naval Infantry Battalion, Detachment BRAVO

   1. SITUATION: At 0832H, one (1) gray-hulled vessel, approximately 45 meters in length,
   bearing markings consistent with China Coast Guard (hull number obscured), was observed
   at grid 14N PB 123-456, bearing 270 degrees, speed approximately 5 knots, heading west.
   Three (3) crew members visible on deck. No weapons displayed.

   2. ACTION TAKEN: Unit assumed observation posture. Vessel was photographed and video-recorded.
   PCG hotline notified at 0838H. No approach made.

   3. ASSESSMENT: Likely patrol vessel operating within Philippine EEZ. No hostile act observed.
   Presence constitutes a sovereignty concern requiring documentation and diplomatic notification.

   4. RECOMMENDATION: Request guidance from AFP WESCOM. Request PCG coordination for monitoring.

   5. ATTACHMENTS: 47 photographs, 12-minute video footage (transmitted separately).
   REPORTED BY: 1LT Juan dela Cruz, Detachment Commander

2. SITUATION REPORT (SITREP)
   Used for: Regular scheduled reporting of unit status to higher command.
   Frequency: As ordered (typically every 6, 12, or 24 hours).

   FORMAT:
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   TO: [Commander / HQ]
   FROM: [Rank, Name, Unit]
   DTG: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   SITREP NUMBER: [Sequential]
   PERIOD COVERED: [From DTG to DTG]
   CLASSIFICATION: [UNCLASSIFIED / RESTRICTED / CONFIDENTIAL]

   1. OWN SITUATION:
      a. Location: [Current grid or AO description]
      b. Strength: [Officers: X / Enlisted: X / Total: X / Effective for duty: X]
      c. Disposition: [How unit is deployed — patrol, static, reserve, etc.]

   2. ENEMY/THREAT SITUATION:
      [Description of enemy or threat forces observed or reported in your AO.
      Include: estimated strength, location, activity, and assessment of intent.
      State "NO CONTACT" or "NO ENEMY ACTIVITY OBSERVED" if none.]

   3. SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITIES (SIGACTS):
      [List all notable events since last SITREP with individual DTGs.
      Include: patrols conducted, contacts made, checkpoints operated, civilian interactions,
      incidents, and any SPOTREPs already transmitted.]

   4. LOGISTICS STATUS:
      a. Ammunition: [State % of basic load remaining]
      b. Rations: [Days of food supply remaining]
      c. Water: [State potable water supply status]
      d. Medical: [Casualties this period — KIA/WIA/Missing. Medical supply status.]
      e. Fuel: [Vehicles/generators — hours or liters remaining]
      f. Equipment: [Any non-mission-capable (NMC) equipment. State what and reason.]

   5. COMMUNICATIONS STATUS:
      [Primary radio: operational / degraded / non-operational]
      [Secondary comms: state status]
      [Any issues affecting comms security or reliability]

   6. ACTIONS TAKEN:
      [Summary of unit actions taken during the reporting period in response to
      threats, incidents, or orders received.]

   7. PLANNED ACTIONS:
      [What the unit intends to do in the next reporting period.]

   8. REQUESTS / REQUIREMENTS:
      [Resupply, reinforcement, medical evacuation, equipment, guidance, or support needed.]

   SUBMITTED BY: [Rank, Name]
   AUTHENTICATION: [Unit authentication code per CEOI]
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

3. CONTACT REPORT (IMMEDIATE)
   Used for: Immediate transmission upon making contact with hostile or unknown armed forces.
   Time: Transmitted WHILE IN CONTACT or within 1 minute of breaking contact.
   Format: Verbal radio transmission — short and clear.

   RADIO FORMAT (SALUTE):
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   S — SIZE:         How many personnel / vehicles / vessels?
   A — ACTIVITY:     What are they doing? (firing, moving, observing)
   L — LOCATION:     Grid coordinates or landmark reference
   U — UNIT:         What unit/group are they? (if identifiable)
   T — TIME:         Exact time of first contact
   E — EQUIPMENT:    Weapons, vehicles, uniforms observed
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

   EXAMPLE TRANSMISSION:
   "ALPHA-6, this is ALPHA-2, CONTACT REPORT, over.
   SALUTE follows:
   SIZE — approximately 15 dismounted personnel.
   ACTIVITY — moving north on foot, armed, appear hostile.
   LOCATION — Grid 14N QB 789-123, treeline east of Route LIMA.
   UNIT — Unknown, wearing civilian clothing.
   TIME — 1312H.
   EQUIPMENT — AK-type rifles, one RPG sighted.
   We are taking fire. Request immediate QRF. Over."

4. CASUALTY REPORT (CASREP)
   Used for: Reporting friendly casualties (KIA, WIA, Missing) to higher command.
   Transmit as soon as tactically feasible — do not delay medical response to file report.

   FORMAT:
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   DTG: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   REPORTING UNIT: [Unit]
   INCIDENT DTG: [When casualty occurred]
   INCIDENT LOCATION: [Grid]

   FOR EACH CASUALTY:
   1. NAME: [Last name, First name, Middle initial]
   2. RANK / SERVICE NUMBER: [Rank / SN]
   3. STATUS: [KIA / WIA-Urgent / WIA-Priority / WIA-Routine / MISSING]
   4. NATURE OF INJURY/CAUSE OF DEATH: [Brief description — GSW, blast injury, illness, etc.]
   5. TREATMENT GIVEN: [First aid administered — tourniquet, wound packing, CPR, etc.]
   6. MEDEVAC REQUESTED: [YES / NO — If yes, state 9-line transmitted or pending]
   7. LOCATION OF CASUALTY NOW: [Still at incident site / en route / at aid station]

   TOTAL CASUALTIES THIS INCIDENT: KIA: [X] / WIA: [X] / MISSING: [X]
   REPORTED BY: [Rank, Name]
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

5. INTELLIGENCE REPORT (INTREP)
   Used for: Reporting intelligence information — enemy movements, informant reports,
   captured documents, suspicious activities of intelligence value.

   FORMAT:
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   CLASSIFICATION: [CONFIDENTIAL / RESTRICTED]
   DTG: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   REPORTING UNIT: [Unit]
   SOURCE: [Observation / Informant / Captured Document / Interrogation / Other]
   SOURCE RELIABILITY: [Reliable / Usually reliable / Unknown / Unreliable]
   INFORMATION ACCURACY: [Confirmed / Probably true / Doubtful / Cannot be assessed]

   1. INFORMATION:
      [State exactly what was observed or reported. Do not evaluate here — just facts.]

   2. LOCATION: [Grid or description where information pertains to]

   3. DTG OF INFORMATION: [When did this occur or when was it reported?]

   4. ANALYSIS:
      [Your assessment of the information's significance and reliability.]

   5. RECOMMENDED ACTION:
      [What intelligence follow-up or operational response is suggested?]

   REPORTED BY: [Rank, Name, Position]
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

---

6. PATROL REPORT (PATROLREP)
   Used for: Debriefing after patrol completion. Filed within 2 hours of returning to base.

   FORMAT:
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   UNIT: [Patrol element designation]
   PATROL LEADER: [Rank, Name]
   DTG DEPARTED: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   DTG RETURNED: [DDMMMYYYY / HHMM]
   PATROL TYPE: [Reconnaissance / Combat / Ambush / Security / Civic Action]
   AREA OF OPERATIONS: [AO name or coordinates of route]

   1. MISSION: [Assigned task]
   2. ROUTE: [Waypoints traversed, in order]
   3. ENEMY ACTIVITY OBSERVED:
      [Describe all signs of enemy or hostile activity: tracks, camps, equipment,
      personnel, firing positions. State grid for each. "NEGATIVE CONTACT" if none.]
   4. CIVILIAN SITUATION:
      [Population mood, key leaders met, concerns raised, suspicious activity.]
   5. KEY TERRAIN OBSERVATIONS:
      [Obstacles, bridges, fords, chokepoints, landing zones, cover and concealment.]
   6. FRIENDLY CASUALTIES: [KIA / WIA / none]
   7. EQUIPMENT STATUS: [Weapons, radios, vehicles — any unserviceable items?]
   8. RECOMMENDATIONS:
      [Follow-up actions, additional patrols needed, intelligence leads to pursue.]

   PATROL LEADER SIGNATURE: ___________________
   -----------------------------------------------------------------------

---

REPORT SUBMISSION REMINDERS

  - Negative reports still required: If nothing happened, submit SITREP stating "NO SIGNIFICANT ACTIVITY."
  - Timely over perfect: A quick, incomplete report transmitted early is more valuable than a
    perfect report transmitted late.
  - Authentication: All radio-transmitted reports require authentication per your unit CEOI.
  - Hard copy: Maintain paper copies of all submitted reports in the unit journal.
  - Photos and video: Label all media with DTG, location, and brief description before transmitting.
